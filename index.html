About Me

Robotics and Machine Learning Engineer

A mechatronics engineering graduate with hands-on experience in machine/deep learning and robotics. Very passionate about cognition and making machines smarter and better in every possible aspect. Always seeking opportunities to imrove my skills by working or conductiong research in the fields of robotics and machine learning. Always open to work on novel unconventional ideas.

My passion about engineering flourshied early in my life, had my first Arduino in grade 8 and learned all about it in three days reading a simplified book about embedded programming. Then I had my first PIC at grade 9. I had the first chance to conduct research in the summer before Thanaweya Amma (grade 12 equivalent). I, somehow, managed to contact the R&D team at FRESH, a local home appliances company, who really motivated me to do this research. I ended up having a patent based on this research named "Piezoelectric Potentiometer" in October 2014. If I'm to judge it now, I would say it was horrible. But it was just my first step.

My first day in college was like a dream to me; I daydreamed all the day of the many things I will do in this place and about that last day in graduation gown celebrating the countless achievements I have achieved. My last day in college was a bit different than I imagined. Graduating in a global pandemic was by no means a thing that I would have imagined. Nevertheless, I managed to achieve all I wanted and dreamed of; Two scholarships, a member of many teams, a team leader, one research grant, honoured by the president (in a team), top 1% on my class, and a published paper.

Thankfully, I worked after graduation, despite the continuotion of the pandemic and the lockdown, as a machine learning engineer and a TA at Ain Shams University. I still, and will always, have the same spirit of wanting to discover and apply novel and unconventional solutions to the problems I face.

I am always looking forward to new opportunities.

Résumé

What do I do now?

I am a machine learning research engineer at Uniparticle. The main task I was hired for is to make Adaptavia, an online authoring tool, smarter and able to provide teachers with a set of tools to create adaptive courses and to do pre and post analysis via simulation, results analysis, and sentiment analysis of given feedback. All is to be done by machines without the need for educational experts throughout the process.

Now, I design, propose, and implement intelligent solutions and tools for our projects. More about what I do at Uniparticle and what I have done before is in my resume and on LinkedIn.
