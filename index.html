About Me

Robotics and Machine Learning Engineer

I am a graduate mechatronics engineer (class of 2020) with hands-on experience in machine learning and robotics. Passionate about increasing the cognitive abilities of machines and robots, making their interaction with their environment smoother and smarter. Currently seeking opportunities to contribute and improve my skills by working or conducting research in robotics and machine learning.

My first day in college was like a dream to me. I daydreamed all the day of the many things I will do in this place and about that last day in graduation gown celebrating the countless achievements I will have achieved by then. My last day in college was a bit different than I imagined; Graduating in a global pandemic was by no means a thing that I would have imagined. I, Nevertheless, managed to achieve all I wanted and dreamed of; Two scholarships, one research grant, a member of many teams, a team leader, honoured by the president (in a team), top 1% on my class, and a published paper.

Thankfully, I worked after graduation, despite the continuotion of the pandemic and the lockdown, as a machine learning engineer and a TA at Ain Shams University. I still have, and will always have, the same spirit of wanting to discover and apply novel and unconventional solutions to the problems I face.

Résumé

What do I do now?

I am a machine learning research engineer at Uniparticle. The main task I was hired for is to make Adaptavia, an online authoring tool, smarter and able to provide teachers with a set of tools to create adaptive courses and to do pre and post analysis via simulation, results analysis, and sentiment analysis of given feedback. All is to be done by machines without the need for educational experts throughout the process.

Now, I design, propose, and implement intelligent solutions and tools for our projects. More about what I do at Uniparticle and what I have done before is in my resume and on LinkedIn.

Projects

IDeepify

A robust deep learning-based ID verification (face matching, OCR for data extraction, ID validation) for Egyptian IDs.

ROS Package: move_base_sequence

A ROS Action server to handle communication with move base action server to navigate through a list of waypoints.

ROS package: Swerve steering ROS controller

A ROS package to control and drive a robot with any set of wheels with any configurations (steerable, steerable with limits, fixed)

ROS package: stable D435i localization

Some modifications on D435i camera parameters that led to a more stable visual localization. The package also utilizes RTABmap for vSLAM.

Autonomous agricultural platform
